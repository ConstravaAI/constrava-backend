Privacy Policy

Constrava provides business dashboard, CRM, analytics, and form-intake tools. This Privacy Policy explains how Constrava collects, uses, stores, and protects information when someone uses the Constrava website, dashboard, CRM demo, private app workspace, or connected form integrations.

Information we collect

Depending on how the product is used, Constrava may collect:

  • Contact information submitted through forms, such as name, email address, phone number, company, and message content.
  • CRM data entered or imported by a user, such as leads, contacts, accounts, deals, tasks, notes, and activity records.
  • Dashboard and analytics event data, such as page views, clicks, lead submissions, traffic sources, device type, and timestamps.
  • Technical data needed to operate the service, such as request logs, error information, and basic browser/device information.

Google user data

If a user connects Google Forms, Constrava may request limited Google account permissions to identify the user and list Google Forms from that user's Google Drive metadata. Constrava uses this access only to help the user choose a Google Form and connect that form to the CRM intake workflow.

Constrava does not sell Google user data. Constrava does not use Google user data for advertising. Constrava does not transfer Google user data to third parties except as necessary to provide or improve user-facing features, comply with law, protect against abuse, or with the user's direction and consent.

Google Form response forwarding is handled by a user-installed Apps Script connected to the user's Google Form response Sheet. That script sends form submission fields to the user's Constrava intake endpoint so they can become CRM lead records.

How we use information

Constrava uses collected information to:

  • Display CRM records, dashboards, reports, and workflow results.
  • Receive and process lead submissions from website forms and connected form tools.
  • Generate analytics summaries and operational reports.
  • Provide support, maintain security, debug issues, and improve the product.

Storage and retention

Some demo data may be stored temporarily in the browser session. Production data may be stored in Constrava's backend systems and databases when configured. Constrava aims to retain information only as long as reasonably necessary to provide the service, maintain records, comply with legal obligations, and support security.

Sharing

Constrava may use trusted infrastructure providers for hosting, email, database, analytics, logging, and security. These providers process information only as needed to operate the service. Constrava does not sell personal information.

User choices

Users can disconnect integrations, stop using connected form forwarding scripts, and request deletion or correction of information where applicable. A user can also revoke Google access from their Google Account permissions page.

Security

Constrava uses reasonable technical and organizational safeguards designed to protect information. No online service can guarantee perfect security, so users should avoid submitting sensitive personal information unless necessary for the business workflow.

Contact

Questions about this Privacy Policy can be sent to constrava@constravaai.com.
