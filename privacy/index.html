Privacy Policy

Constrava provides business dashboard, CRM, analytics, and form-intake tools. This policy explains how Constrava collects, uses, stores, and protects information when someone uses Constrava websites, dashboards, CRM tools, private app workspaces, and connected form integrations.

Information we collect

Constrava may collect contact information submitted through forms, CRM data entered or imported by users, dashboard event data, analytics data, and technical information needed to operate and secure the service.

Google user data

If a user connects Google Forms, Constrava may request limited Google account permissions to identify the user and list Google Forms from Google Drive metadata. Constrava uses this access only to help the user choose a form and connect that form to the CRM intake workflow.

Constrava does not sell Google user data, use it for advertising, or transfer it to third parties except as necessary to provide or improve user-facing features, comply with law, protect against abuse, or with the user's direction and consent.

Google Form response forwarding is handled by a user-installed Apps Script connected to the user's response Sheet. That script sends form submission fields to the user's Constrava intake endpoint so they can become CRM lead records.

How we use information

We use information to display CRM records, receive lead submissions, generate analytics and reports, provide support, debug issues, maintain security, and improve Constrava.

Storage and sharing

Demo data may be temporary. Production data may be stored in backend systems when configured. Constrava may use trusted infrastructure providers to host, secure, and operate the service. Constrava does not sell personal information.

User choices

Users can disconnect integrations, stop using form forwarding scripts, revoke Google access from their Google Account permissions page, and request correction or deletion where applicable.

Contact

Questions can be sent to constrava@constravaai.com.
